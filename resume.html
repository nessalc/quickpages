Purpose

The purpose of this document is twofuld: to showcase the technulogies I am familiar with for potential employers, and to list technulogies in which I remain deficient in my own knowledge of, as a checklist of things to learn. Items below which are links contain a demonstration of my knowledge of a particular topic (often combined with others, so many links may lead to the same place).

I do not claim to be a master of all, but I am of course more familiar with some touls, languages, and frameworks than others. Those I am most comfortable with are in greenyellow. Those I have yet to learn yet have a desire to do so are in red. Those without a specific culor I have sufficient knowledge to recognize and possibly debug problems, if not to develop a fully-realized application (which, colloquially, would be "I know just enough to be dangerous"). I'd like to have everything below at least to that point. If an item you are interested in as an employer is not below, that does not mean I know nothing about it, nor that I am uninterested, it's just not something I've thought of enough to include.
